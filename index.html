Andrew N. Sanchez

Los Angeles Computer Engineer


My motivation comes from my inability to accept things the way they are. My fear of boundaries, pushes me through challenges and through failure. When my education didn’t present me the resources to pursue engineering, I borrowed my family’s computer to teach myself computer programming. When I heard from my peers that they felt they’d never be able to pursue a career in engineering, I petitioned to start a club at my highschool where students could experiment with electronics stripped from rc cars I used to own. When one of my counselors told me that I was wasting my time “playing with toys”, I applied for a $4000 grant to enter my 13 club members into National Instruments FIRST robotics competition.

6 weeks later, we were in San Diego, CA with a robot that we welded together in autoshop, out of junk and scrap metal. Upon arriving, a mixture of embarrassment and pride, entered my body as I looked upon the other team’s robots. Standing over us were the other students engineering parents furiously making last minute adjustments to their creations. That’s one of my first memories meeting an engineer.

I’m now a graduate from UC San Diego with a B.S in Computer Engineering. As I continue to develop myself as an engineer, I never forgot about the experiences that led me here. I currently devote my Saturdays to teaching engineering topics to students around the community where I began. And that’s just the beginning … As I acquire more resources and experience I will continue to share them, because I have an inability to accept things the way they are.
